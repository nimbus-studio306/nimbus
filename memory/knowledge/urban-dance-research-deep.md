## 1. Soul Train

**Dates:**
- Local premiere: August 17, 1970 (WCIU-TV Chicago)
- National: October 2, 1971
- End: March 27, 2006

**Don Cornelius:**
- Born: September 27, 1936, Chicago
- Died: February 1, 2012 (suicide)
- Sign-off: "Love, peace, and soul!"

**Famous dancers:**
- Damita Jo Freeman — energy, style, influential
- Patricia Davis — grace and style
- Don "Campbellock" Campbell — locking showcased
- Little Joe Chism — popping/locking influence

## 2. Disco Demolition Night (July 12, 1979)

**Steve Dahl:**
- Shock jock at WLUP-FM Chicago
- Previously fired from WDAI (disco station)
- "Disco Sucks" campaign

**What happened:**
- White Sox + Dahl promotional event
- Fans brought disco records for discount
- Crate of records exploded between doubleheader games
- Fans stormed field → second game forfeited

**Racial undertones (DEBATED):**
- Disco = Black, Latino, LGBTQ+ associated
- Backlash interpretation vs. "just musical taste"
- Complex mix of factors

## 3. Hip-Hop Founding

**August 11, 1973 party:**
- Location: 1520 Sedgwick Avenue, Bronx
- Hosts: DJ Kool Herc + sister Cindy Campbell
- Purpose: Back-to-school fundraiser
- Innovation: Extended the "break" using two turntables

**Who coined "hip-hop"? (CONTESTED)**
- Lovebug Starski
- Cowboy (Keith Wiggins) — teasing soldier friend
- DJ Hollywood
- Likely evolved organically, no single inventor

**Sugarhill Gang controversy:**
- Assembled by Sylvia Robinson (not from scene)
- Lyrics borrowed from Grandmaster Caz (Cold Crush Brothers)
- Accusations of commercializing culture

## 4. NYC Blackout (July 13, 1977)

- Lightning strikes → ~25 hour outage
- Widespread looting
- Block parties emerged
- **Equipment looting story**: Widely cited but hard to verify
  - Anecdotal evidence exists
  - May be romanticized/overemphasized
  - Present with nuance

## 5. Michael Jackson Influences

**Teachers:**
- Electric Boogaloos — popping, boogaloo
- Gerard "Kool G" Brown

**Moonwalk/backslide origins:**
- Bill Bailey (1950s tap dancer) — similar move
- James Brown, Jeffrey Daniel (Shalamar) variations
- MJ popularized globally: Motown 25 (1983)

**Bob Fosse "The Little Prince" (1974):**
- Fosse as "The Snake" — isolations, angular movements
- Possible influence on MJ

**Electric Boogaloos relationship:**
- Appeared in "Thriller", "Beat It"
- Toured together
- Brought popping to mainstream

## 6. Paris Is Burning

- **Sundance Grand Jury Prize: 1991** ✓
- Explored race, class, gender, sexuality in ballroom scene
- Major impact on voguing awareness

**Madonna "Vogue" controversy:**
- Directly inspired by ballroom culture
- Appropriation debate ongoing
- Brought voguing mainstream but without credit/compensation

## 7. House Music Origins

**Frankie Knuckles ("Godfather of House"):**
- Resident DJ at The Warehouse, Chicago (1977-1982)
- Previously NYC disco scene (influenced by Larry Levan)

**"House music" name origins (DEBATED):**
1. From The Warehouse club ("play that track from the house")
2. Created in people's homes with affordable gear

**NYC connection:**
- House evolved from disco
- Paradise Garage (NYC) + Warehouse (Chicago) = foundational
