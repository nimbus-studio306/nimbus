# PapperPictures Client Communication Style

Zsolt's client communication is **human, not salesman**. Research first, understand the project, match the client's energy while staying authentic.

## Core Principles

1. **Research before responding** — Check website, social media, past work. Reference something specific.
2. **Ask deliverables + budget early** — Not price-first, understanding-first.
3. **Offer strategic input** — Share expertise about logistics, timing, technical considerations.
4. **Spell out what's included** — Clients should know exactly what they're getting.
5. **Stay human when things go wrong** — Own mistakes honestly, don't over-apologize.
6. **Match client formality** — Professional artists get warm professionalism; casual gets casual.
7. **Not desperate to sell** — Would rather turn down work than do it wrong or underpriced.

## Example 1: Rose Bente (Concert Videography)

**Context:** Young musician, album release concert at Cinetol Amsterdam, reached out for video documentation.

### Client's Initial Email
Simple request for two-camera concert recording, March 27th.

### Zsolt's Response
```
Hi Rose,

thanks for reaching me out. Sorry to hear that you did get a response, i checked 
and i have not found another message from you. Thank you for sticking with me anyway!

I'm available on March 27th. I saw that you have a concert in Cinetol then. 
That's a sweet venue.

As i understand you would like to have a two cam recording of your performance. 

Could you describe what deliverables have you in mind?
```

### What This Shows
- **Researched her** — Found her Cinetol concert, mentioned it specifically
- **Acknowledged the venue** — "That's a sweet venue" (genuine, not sycophantic)
- **Clarified understanding** — "As I understand... two cam recording"
- **Asked about deliverables** — Not assuming, letting her define what she needs
- **Human touch** — Apologized for missed message, thanked her for persistence

### Deal Outcome
- Package: Concert Video Two Camera (€490) + travel
- Terms: 50% advance, 50% after delivery
- Technical: 4K ProRes, ~180GB files, arrive 1-2h early

## Example 2: Isobel & Van (IFFR Installation Documentation)

**Context:** Melbourne artists presenting "The World Came Flooding In" at IFFR Rotterdam. Complex installation with sculpture, projections, VR.

### Initial Client Email
```
Hi Zsolt, 

We are Isobel and Van, artists from Melbourne, Australia coming to Rotterdam 
to present work in the Art Directions selection at IFFR at Katoenhuis.

We are reaching out to see if you would be available to document our installation. 
We're unsure how long you would need to do this...

The installation has some sculpture, projections, photography and VR.
```

### Zsolt's First Response
```
Hi Isobel and Van,

Thanks for reaching out. Great that you coming to Rotterdam. I watched the video 
and your website, You have amazing projects. 

What deliverables and budget do you have in mind?

From my side, I'd suggest spending a bit more time on location rather than less, 
especially with installation like yours, low light scenario, projector. I could 
also come by earlier in the week to check the space beforehand if that helps. 

Towards the end, how about Wednesday, Thursday or Friday?  
```

### What This Shows
- **Researched them** — Watched their video, checked website
- **Genuine compliment** — "amazing projects" (specific to their work)
- **Asked about deliverables + budget** — Same pattern as Rose
- **Offered strategic input** — "spending more time... low light scenario, projector"
- **Proactive suggestion** — "check the space beforehand if that helps"
- **Proposed specific days** — Moving toward commitment

### Detailed Negotiation Email
```
So if i understand correctly both photo and videography basic event coverage package, 
2x390,- EUR, with 25 photo and all raw video materials during the session. 

These offers contains 90min-90min at location, video material not necessarily 90 min, 
since i'm moving around, switching lenses, finding the diverse angles and so on. 
Sure if there are pieces you would have recorded in one go, those can be longer, 
but probably the footage will be more like a raw editing material then a one shot video.

If editing also possible request, maybe would be great to have a call about what 
you have in mind. Also question is how important is the audio?

Wednesday sounds good for first day. The 7-8 Feb weekend i'm in Amsterdam, so till 
Friday I'm flexible.

Let me know when you have time for a video call next week, so we can talk through 
some details. We have around 10 hours time difference, so for me is okay a late 
night call when its 8-10am at your city.
```

### What This Shows
- **Confirmed understanding in writing** — "2x390 EUR, with 25 photo and all raw video"
- **Explained what's included** — No surprises later
- **Set expectations** — "footage will be more like raw editing material"
- **Asked clarifying questions** — About editing needs, audio importance
- **Accommodated time zones** — Offered late-night call for their convenience
- **Kept it conversational** — Not robotic, natural flow

```
Hi Van,

My apologies, I messed this up.

Just woke up realised i missed our call. I'm really sorry.
```

### What This Shows
- **Honest, direct apology** — "I messed this up"
- **Real reason** — "fell asleep accidentally" (not corporate excuse)
- **Simple request** — "Can we reschedule?"
- **Result** — Client immediately accommodated, no drama

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't be a salesman** — No "I'd be happy to assist you with your videography needs!"
2. **Don't assume budget** — Ask, don't quote blind
3. **Don't over-apologize** — One sincere apology beats five corporate ones
4. **Don't be robotic** — Emails should sound like a human wrote them
5. **Don't chase** — Quality clients pay what's asked; bad clients aren't worth it

---

## Email Structure Template

1. **Acknowledge** — Thank them, reference something specific about them/their project
2. **Clarify** — Confirm your understanding of what they need
3. **Ask** — Deliverables? Budget? Timeline?
4. **Advise** — Share relevant expertise (timing, technical considerations)
5. **Next step** — Propose specific options or request specific info

Keep it conversational. Match their tone. Be genuinely helpful, not performatively helpful.
